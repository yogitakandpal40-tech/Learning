Concept of Consumer Behaviour

Consumer behaviour refers to the study of individuals, groups, or organizations and the processes they use to select, secure, use, and dispose of products and services to satisfy their needs and wants. It helps businesses understand customer preferences and buying decisions.

Scope of Consumer Behaviour

The scope of consumer behaviour includes studying how consumers make purchasing decisions, factors influencing their choices, the impact of advertising, and the role of culture, social class, and personal characteristics in shaping consumer preferences.

Implications of Consumer Behaviour

Understanding consumer behaviour helps businesses create effective marketing strategies, develop suitable products, improve customer satisfaction, and increase sales. It also helps in identifying new market opportunities and gaining competitive advantage.

Consumer Decision-Making Process

  • Problem Recognition: Identifying a need or desire.
  • Information Search: Collecting information about products.
  • Evaluation of Alternatives: Comparing different options.
  • Purchase Decision: Selecting and buying the product.
  • Post-Purchase Behavior: Evaluating satisfaction after purchase.

Levels of Consumer Decision-Making

  • Routine Response Behavior: Low involvement, frequent purchases.
  • Limited Decision-Making: Moderate involvement, few comparisons.
  • Extensive Decision-Making: High involvement, detailed evaluation.

Types of Consumer Decision-Making

  • Complex Buying Behavior: High involvement, major differences.
  • Dissonance-Reducing Buying Behavior: High involvement, fewer differences.
  • Habitual Buying Behavior: Low involvement, routine purchase.
  • Variety-Seeking Buying Behavior: Low involvement, brand switching.
