Consumer Behavior Notes

Unit 1: Introduction to Consumer Behavior

  • Definition: Study of individuals, groups, and organizations and the processes they use to select, use, and dispose of products.
  • Importance: Helps marketers understand consumer needs and decision-making.
  • Scope: Consumer motivation, perception, learning, attitudes, personality, and lifestyle.

Unit 2: Consumer Motivation & Involvement

  • Motivation: Inner drive to satisfy needs (Physiological, Safety, Social, Esteem, Self-actualization).
  • Theories: Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs.
  • Consumer Involvement: Level of interest/relevance; High involvement (careful evaluation), Low involvement (routine).

Unit 3: Personality & Self-Concept

  • Personality: Traits influencing buying behavior (innovative, risk-taking, sociable).
  • Self-Concept: Consumer’s perception of self (Actual self, Ideal self, Social self).
  • Impact: Self-image influences brand choice, product perception, and advertising effectiveness.

Unit 4: Perception, Learning & Memory

  • Perception: Process of selecting, organizing, and interpreting information (Exposure, Attention, Interpretation).
  • Learning: Changes in behavior due to experience (Classical conditioning, Operant conditioning, Observational learning).
  • Memory: Retention of knowledge (Sensory, Short-term, Long-term).

Unit 5: Attitudes & Information Processing

  • Attitudes: Learned predisposition to respond (Cognitive, Affective, Behavioral).
  • Changing Attitudes: Cognitive (facts), Affective (emotions), Behavioral (trial/experience).
  • Information Processing: How consumers process info (Exposure → Attention → Comprehension → Acceptance → Retention).
